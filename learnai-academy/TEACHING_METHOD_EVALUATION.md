#### **B. Cognitive Load Theory**

**Principles:**
1. **Intrinsic Load** - Complexity of content
   - Break complex topics into smaller chunks
   - Present one concept at a time
   - Use worked examples

2. **Extraneous Load** - Unnecessary distractions
   - Remove irrelevant information
   - Clean, focused presentations
   - Minimal text on slides

3. **Germane Load** - Processing for learning
   - Encourage active processing
   - Use visual aids effectively
   - Provide opportunities for practice

#### **C. Multimedia Learning Principles (Mayer's Principles)**

1. **Multimedia Principle** - Words + pictures > words alone
2. **Spatial Contiguity** - Text near corresponding graphics
3. **Temporal Contiguity** - Words and pictures simultaneously
4. **Coherence** - Exclude extraneous material
5. **Modality** - Spoken words > printed words (for animations)
6. **Redundancy** - Avoid redundant text and narration
7. **Segmenting** - Break content into learner-paced segments
8. **Pre-training** - Teach key concepts before main lesson
9. **Signaling** - Highlight essential material
10. **Personalization** - Use conversational style
